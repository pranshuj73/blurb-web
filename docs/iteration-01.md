# Iteration 01

## Goal
Evaluate clarity of the landing page structure.

## Component Analysis

### Hero Section
**Current headline**
- "Show a QR. Start the conversation."

**Strengths**
- Short
- Memorable
- Human tone

**Weaknesses**
- Does not explain the product
- Could refer to many products
- Missing context: digital card

**Improvement**
- New candidate: **"Your digital card. One scan away."**
- Reason: Immediate product definition

**Alternative candidates**
- "Share your site in one scan."
- "Turn your link into a QR card."
- "Networking without usernames."

**Decision**
- Primary hero: **"Share your site in one scan."**
- Subtext: "Blurb turns your website or socials into a QR card you can show instantly."

### Navigation
**Current**
- Contact only

**Observation**
- Extremely minimal but slightly suspicious for a product.

**Improvement**
- Add nav items: Flow, Privacy, Contact
- Reason: Legitimacy + orientation

### Flow Section
**Strengths**
- Clear three-step structure

**Weaknesses**
- Too abstract visually

**Improvement**
- Each step must show a real UI state:
  - Create card → link input UI
  - Tap card → QR fullscreen UI
  - Scan → phone camera scanning QR

### Feature Section
**Observation**
- Features overlap with flow

**Improvement**
- Replace with benefits instead of features:
  - No usernames to remember
  - No phone number exchange
  - Works with any camera

### QR Showcase
**Observation**
- Strong concept but redundant

**Improvement**
- Convert to live demonstration section:
  - Interactive QR
  - Hover → preview page

### CTA
**Observation**
- Too formal

**Improvement**
- Replace "Questions?" with **"Want to try it?"**

### Footer
**Observation**
- Too empty

**Improvement**
- Add: Privacy, GitHub, Contact

## Improvements
- Tighten hero definition and subtext
- Add basic nav structure for credibility
- Replace abstract visuals with real UI states

## Decision
- **Hero headline: "Share your site in one scan."**

## Direction for Iteration 2
- Focus on hero visual hierarchy, typography scale, and spacing rhythm.
